IVF Treatment

What Is In Vitro Fertilization (IVF)?

In vitro fertilization (IVF) is an assisted reproductive technology in which eggs are fertilized with sperm outside the body. The resulting embryos are cultured and then transferred into the uterus.

IVF is used when natural conception is difficult and allows careful selection and management of embryos under controlled laboratory conditions.

Who May Benefit from IVF?

  • Unexplained infertility
  • Fallopian tube disorders
  • Endometriosis
  • Male factor infertility
  • Advanced maternal age
  • Repeated unsuccessful fertility treatments

※ Treatment suitability is determined after medical evaluation.

IVF Treatment Process

01

Ovarian Stimulation

Hormonal medications stimulate the ovaries to produce multiple eggs.

02

Egg Retrieval

Eggs are collected using a minimally invasive procedure under ultrasound guidance.

03

Fertilization

Eggs are fertilized with sperm in the laboratory using conventional IVF or ICSI.

04

Embryo Culture

Embryos are cultured for several days to assess development.

05

Embryo Transfer

Selected embryos are transferred into the uterus at the appropriate timing.

IVF Treatment Options

Conventional IVF

Eggs and sperm are co-incubated to allow natural fertilization in the laboratory.

ICSI

Intracytoplasmic sperm injection (ICSI) involves injecting a single sperm into an egg.

Frozen Embryo Transfer

Cryopreserved embryos may be transferred in a later cycle under optimal conditions.

Important Considerations

IVF does not guarantee pregnancy or live birth. Success rates depend on age, embryo quality, underlying medical conditions, and treatment history.

Emotional, physical, and financial aspects should be carefully considered before starting treatment.
