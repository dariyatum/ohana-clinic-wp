Preimplantation Genetic Testing

PGT-A / PGT-SR

What is Preimplantation Genetic Testing?

Preimplantation Genetic Testing (PGT) is a technique used during in vitro fertilization (IVF) to examine embryos before transfer. It allows the selection of embryos with normal chromosomal structure.

PGT is performed by analyzing a small number of cells taken from the embryo at the blastocyst stage and does not affect normal embryo development.

Types of PGT

PGT-A

Preimplantation Genetic Testing for Aneuploidy

PGT-A examines the number of chromosomes in embryos. It helps identify embryos with chromosomal abnormalities, which may reduce the risk of miscarriage.

PGT-SR

Preimplantation Genetic Testing for Structural Rearrangements

PGT-SR is recommended for patients who carry chromosomal structural abnormalities, such as translocations or inversions.

Who May Benefit from PGT?

  • Repeated implantation failure
  • Recurrent pregnancy loss
  • Advanced maternal age
  • Known chromosomal abnormalities (PGT-SR)
  • Previous IVF cycles with poor outcomes

※ PGT is not recommended for all patients. A detailed consultation with a physician is required.

PGT Treatment Process

01

IVF & Embryo Culture

Embryos are cultured to the blastocyst stage.

02

Embryo Biopsy

A small number of cells are safely collected.

03

Genetic Analysis

Chromosomal analysis is performed at a specialized laboratory.

04

Embryo Transfer

Genetically suitable embryos are selected for transfer.

Important Considerations

PGT does not guarantee pregnancy or a healthy birth. Results may vary depending on age, embryo quality, and individual medical conditions.

Ethical guidelines and national regulations are strictly followed when offering PGT at Keiai Clinic.
