Egg Freezing

Social Adaptation

What Is Egg Freezing?

Egg freezing (oocyte cryopreservation) is a fertility preservation method that allows women to store their eggs for future use. Social egg freezing is chosen for non-medical reasons, such as career planning or personal circumstances.

By freezing eggs at a younger age, it may be possible to preserve fertility potential for the future.

Who May Consider Social Egg Freezing?

  • Women who wish to delay pregnancy for personal or career reasons
  • Those who are not currently planning pregnancy
  • Women concerned about age-related fertility decline
  • Those seeking future reproductive options

※ Egg freezing does not guarantee future pregnancy. Individual consultation is required.

Egg Freezing Process

01

Initial Consultation

Medical history review, fertility assessment, and explanation of the procedure.

02

Ovarian Stimulation

Hormonal medication is used to stimulate egg development.

03

Egg Retrieval

Eggs are collected through a minor procedure under medical supervision.

04

Vitrification & Storage

Eggs are frozen using advanced cryopreservation and stored securely.

Potential Benefits

Reproductive Flexibility

Allows more time to make life decisions without immediate fertility pressure.

Preservation of Egg Quality

Eggs frozen at a younger age may maintain better reproductive potential.

Future Treatment Options

Frozen eggs may be used later with IVF when pregnancy is desired.

Important Considerations

Social egg freezing is not a guarantee of future pregnancy. Success depends on age at freezing, number of eggs stored, and individual health factors.

We encourage patients to fully understand the benefits, limitations, costs, and emotional aspects before proceeding.
